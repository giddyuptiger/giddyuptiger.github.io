Futon Privacy Policy

Effective date: May 19, 2026

Futon is a Japanese-language learning app. It tracks what you're studying so it can space your reviews intelligently, and it generates example sentences and short conversations using an AI model. This policy explains what data Futon collects, who it's shared with, and what your rights are.

Who we are

Futon is built and operated by Jeremy Irons (sole developer). Contact: jeremydirons@gmail.com.

What we collect

Account information

  • Email address — used for sign-in. Authentication is handled by Supabase Auth.
  • Username (optional) and display name

Learning data

  • Your study progress — for each item you're learning, the spaced-repetition state (FSRS algorithm parameters, mastery score, scheduled review intervals)
  • Review history — your answers, the format tested (recognition, recall, etc.), response times, and which items you tend to confuse with which
  • Your JLPT target level (N5 through N1, or custom)
  • Your retention target (how often you want to remember items correctly)

Personalization

  • Your interests (a list of topics you've told the app you care about — e.g., food, travel — used to personalize generated example sentences)
  • Whether your profile is set to public (a single boolean)
  • Whether you've opted in to "squad" matching (a single boolean)

AI conversation history

When you chat with the AI tutor or have it generate example sentences, your messages and the model's responses are stored so you can return to the conversation later. Generated sentences may be cached server-side and reused across users when the same content would be appropriate (e.g., for a given known-vocabulary set), but your own message history is private to your account.

Subscription

  • Your subscription tier (free, trial, pro, or lifetime) and trial expiry date if applicable. Payment is processed by Apple's App Store — we never see your card or Apple ID.

Who can see your data

  • Your learning progress, review history, conversation history, and account info are private to you. They are stored under your account with row-level security so other users can't read them.
  • If you set your profile to public, other users can see your username, display name, and high-level progress (e.g., JLPT level), but not your individual reviews or messages.
  • If you opt in to squad matching, a nightly job groups you with similar learners. Your squad-mates see your activity feed (e.g., "studied 30 minutes today") but not the content of your reviews or conversations.

Third-party services we use

  • Supabase — hosts the database, authentication, file storage, edge functions, and realtime channels. All your data lives here.
  • Anthropic (Claude API) — when you chat with the AI tutor or request a generated example sentence, the relevant context (your message, your known vocabulary, your interests) is sent to Anthropic's API to generate a response. Anthropic does not use this content to train their models when accessed via the API. See Anthropic's privacy policy.
  • Apple App Store — handles all subscription payments. We only receive a tier and expiry status; we never see payment details.

That's the entire list. Futon does not use advertising SDKs, attribution trackers, or third-party analytics. We do not sell or rent your data, and we do not use your data to train AI models.

How long we keep your data

Your account, learning state, review history, and conversation history are kept as long as your account exists. When you delete your account, all of this is removed from our database (cascade delete). Generated sentences that have been cached for reuse across the system are anonymous and are kept indefinitely. Backups may retain personal data for up to 30 days before being overwritten.

Your rights

  • Access — your learning data is visible inside the app. Email us for a full export.
  • Correction — you can edit your profile, interests, and settings in the app.
  • Deletion — you can delete your account in settings, or by email. Specific conversations can also be cleared from the app.
  • Opt out — you can turn off public profile and squad matching at any time.

If you live in the EU, UK, or California, you have additional rights under GDPR, UK GDPR, and CCPA — including portability and the right to complain to your local data protection authority. Email us to exercise any of these.

Children

Futon is not directed at children under 13 (or 16 in the EU). We do not knowingly collect data from children. If we learn that a child has signed up, we will delete the account.

Changes to this policy

If we change this policy, we'll update the effective date at the top. Material changes will be surfaced in the app before they take effect.
